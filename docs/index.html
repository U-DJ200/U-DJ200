Built exclusively for the Pioneer DDJ-200

Most DJ software treats the DDJ-200 as a generic MIDI controller. U-DJ200 is designed around it from the ground up — every jog wheel, performance pad, EQ knob, filter, and fader is mapped and tuned specifically for this hardware.

Made for techno, house, trance & every dance genre

U-DJ200 is tuned for DJs across techno, house, tech house, trance, progressive, EDM, electro, drum & bass, dubstep, and any other electronic or club genre — with fast, accurate beat detection and tight sync built for four-on-the-floor music.

Key features

  • Full Pioneer DDJ-200 hardware mapping — every control works out of the box
  • AI stem separation — isolate and mute/solo vocals, drums, bass and other instruments live
  • Beat-synced cues, hot cues and loops that snap cleanly to the beat grid
  • Tempo & phase sync for tight, professional transitions
  • High-quality audio engine — clean pitch shifting, crossfades, and dithered output
  • Local track library with automatic BPM and beat-grid analysis

System requirements

  • macOS: 14 (Sonoma) or later — built and tested on macOS 26.5
  • Mac: Apple Silicon (M1 or later) — built and tested on Apple M4
  • RAM: 8 GB minimum (16 GB recommended for AI stem separation)
  • Hardware: Pioneer DDJ-200 controller connected via USB

Get started

  1. Download U-DJ200.dmg from the Releases page
  2. Drag U-DJ200.app into your Applications folder
  3. Connect your Pioneer DDJ-200 via USB and launch the app

Frequently asked questions

Is U-DJ200 free?
U-DJ200 is available as a free 90-day trial. The app shows a countdown of your remaining trial days, and all features are available during the trial.

Does it work with controllers other than the DDJ-200?
No — U-DJ200 is built exclusively for the Pioneer DDJ-200, with every control mapped and tuned specifically for this hardware.

What Mac do I need?
An Apple Silicon Mac (M1 or later) running macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later, with at least 8 GB RAM (16 GB recommended for AI stem separation).

Why does macOS warn me when I open it?
U-DJ200 isn't notarized by Apple, so Gatekeeper blocks it on first launch. Right-click the app in Applications, choose Open, then confirm — you only need to do this once.
